## Slide 2: The $50 Billion Access to Justice Crisis

**75% of civil rights cases involve pro se litigants** who face procedural complexity designed for trained attorneys. The system creates insurmountable barriers through three mechanisms:

**Information Asymmetry**: Legal research tools (Westlaw, LexisNexis) cost $3,000-$10,000 annually, creating knowledge monopolies that favor institutional defendants with unlimited budgets.

**Qualified Immunity Doctrine**: Requires plaintiffs to identify "clearly established" precedent with factually similar circumstances—a standard that effectively immunizes novel misconduct even when constitutional violations are obvious. Courts dismiss 57% of Section 1983 cases on qualified immunity grounds before reaching the merits.

**Procedural Warfare**: Federal courts increasingly abstain through Younger, Rooker-Feldman, and Colorado River doctrines, forcing litigants into state courts that may be hostile or complicit. Discovery rules theoretically provide evidence access, but defendants routinely assert privilege claims and produce minimal responsive documents.

**Market Opportunity**: 30,000+ Section 1983 cases filed annually, $2.5B in civil rights settlements, growing demand for legal tech solutions addressing systemic inequality.

## Slide 5: Technical Architecture - Multi-Agent Swarm Processing

**Sector Swarm Processing**: Execute all agents within a division simultaneously against a single document, generating comprehensive multi-agent reports in minutes. Parallel execution reduces total processing time from hours to minutes while providing complete coverage.

**Technology Stack**: React 19 + TypeScript frontend, Express + tRPC backend with end-to-end type safety, Drizzle ORM with MySQL/TiDB, Manus Forge API for LLM inference, S3-compatible storage for documents.

**Pattern Recognition Engine**: Analyzes frequency of violations (how many times similar violations occurred), consistency of actors (whether same officials involved in multiple incidents), temporal distribution (violations over time or in clusters), statistical significance (frequency exceeds random chance). Transforms anecdotal evidence into statistical proof.

**Precedent Mining**: Natural language processing enables semantic search finding cases with analogous fact patterns even when different terminology used. Automated citation extraction identifies holdings, reasoning, and subsequent treatment without manual review. Prioritizes controlling circuit precedent, checks for Supreme Court cases, identifies circuit splits.

**Output Integration**: All agents cite sources in Bluebook format with case name, citation, and holding. All agents provide step-by-step reasoning. All agents link to free sources (Justia, CourtListener) ensuring accessibility. Consistent formatting enables combining outputs from multiple agents into unified legal arguments.
